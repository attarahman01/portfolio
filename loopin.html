Loopin

Loopin is a mobile app designed to help parents of neurodivergent children build stronger daily routines and emotional connection through simple, supportive interactions.

Visit Figma Business Proposal
Industry Health
Client Fusion
Role UI/UX Designer
Duration 12 Weeks

My Role

As a UI/UX Designer, I was involved throughout the project, from research and competitive analysis to visual design and system thinking.

I worked on user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity screens, with a focus on the dashboard, notifications experience, and shared design system components. I also contributed to branding assets used for presentations and promotion.

Marketing Analysis

Raising a child with ADHD can be overwhelming. In Canada, about 5% of children are diagnosed (over 500,000), with boys more commonly affected (CADDRA, 2023). Parents often feel unsupported and unsure how to manage emotions and routines (ADHD Parent Surveys, 2022).

Competitor Focus

Most ADHD apps, like Goally, Joon, and Brili, focus on task management but overlook emotional support and parent-child connection.

The LOOPIN Difference

LOOPIN fills this gap with Moodies (emotional check-ins), Loopin Moments (bonding activities), and CalmNest (parent self-care), helping families stay on track and build stronger connections.

The Problem

Parents and children were struggling not because of a lack of care, but because daily routines and emotional communication were hard to manage consistently.

01. Kids

Kids feel misunderstood, overwhelmed, and are constantly struggling with daily expectations.

02. Parents

Parents feel overwhelmed, disconnected, and unsupported in their daily caregiving roles.

The Solution

Moodies

Allows children to express emotions quickly through emojis or voice notes, giving parents immediate emotional context.

Calmnest

Provides parents with simple self check tools to pause, reflect, and manage stress during busy routines.

Looping Moments

Encourages short, low pressure activities designed to strengthen parent child bonding without screen overload.

Design Process

01. Research

Analyzed ADHD parenting apps to understand routine management and daily challenges. Focused on creating a calm, low-friction experience.

02. Exploration

Explored layouts and interactions for routines and mood tracking. Prioritized clear hierarchy, simple navigation, and friendly visuals.

03. Refinement

Refined layouts and components to reduce cognitive load and ensure consistency across all touchpoints.

Usability & Testing

We conducted usability testing to evaluate how users interact with Loopin, focusing on clarity, efficiency, and ease of use. The goal was to ensure that key tasks like managing routines and tracking moods could be completed intuitively.

Dashboard Design Iterations

First Iteration

The initial dashboard displayed routines, mood updates, and activity cards simultaneously. Feedback showed the layout felt visually dense and overwhelming.

Final Iteration

Reorganized to prioritize daily routines and mood indicators. Refined hierarchy and spacing so parents could quickly understand the day's context.
