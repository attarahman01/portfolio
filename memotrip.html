MemoTrip

A mobile travel app focused on helping travelers discover authentic local experiences through community insight and smart route planning.

Visit Figma
Industry Travel
Client Personal Project
Role UI/UX Designer
Duration 8 Weeks

My Role

As the UI/UX Designer on MemoTrip, I worked across research, concept development, and visual design.

My responsibilities included user research, competitive analysis, persona creation, user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity interface design. I led the design of the dashboard, notifications experience, and route builder interactions, and contributed to the design system and branding assets used for presentation and promotion.

The Problem

Travelers waste hours piecing together "authentic" itineraries from generic blog posts and scattered reviews. Current apps either offer rigid tourist traps or overwhelming lists without context, leaving users stressed rather than excited.

The Solution

A platform designed to prioritize community insight and flexible planning.

Authentic Discovery

Surfaces local recommendations shared by people who live there, not generic tourist lists.

Create Itinerary

Creates flexible travel routes based on interests, time, and location.

Share Experience

Builds confidence through shared experiences from verified contributors.

Design Process

01. Research

User interviews, competitive analysis, and travel behavior research.

02. Define

Synthesized insights into persons, user journeys, and product goals.

03. Design

Wireframes, interaction flows, and high-fidelity UI.

04. Validate

Usability testing and iteration based on feedback.

Key Decisions

Strategizing for a better travel experience.

01. Prioritizing "Vibe"

Decision: Replaced 5-star ratings with "Vibe Tags" (e.g., 'Quiet & Cozy').

Why: Travelers choose spots based on mood, not just objective quality.

02. "Live Guide"

Decision: Context-aware home screen based on time/location.

Why: Travelers need immediate, relevant suggestions without digging.

03. Route Builder

Decision: Routes as "loose collections" rather than strict timelines.

Why: Rigid schedules cause anxiety; flexibility allows for spontaneity.

Outcome & Learnings

MemoTrip focuses on mood, context, and local insight for smarter travel discovery. Designs balance guidance and flexibility, helping travelers explore without overwhelm.

Vibe Tags

Outperformed star ratings, helping users decide faster.

Mood-based Discovery

Made planning lighter and more intuitive.

Live Guide

Valued for adapting in real time to location and schedule.

Clear Interface

Refining spacing, typography, and actions improved clarity and confidence.

Emotional Context

Matters more than raw data in travel decisions.
